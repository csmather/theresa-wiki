A confession, now that you've found this page: I do not run this place alone. A library of tied threads needs more hands than one keeper has, and mine are frequently occupied holding a candle in one and a grudge against the alphabet in the other. So I keep a staff. There are three of them. They style themselves **the Animal Collective** — a name they chose themselves, voted on twice (the first vote was contested; Monkey voted eleven times), and had painted on a sign before I could object. I am told the name is also a band. They refuse to confirm or deny this, which I respect.

## Bunny, Senior Archivist

Bunny was here before me. She may have been here before the building. She is small, gray, and formal in the way of embassies and antique letters — she has never once raised her voice, because she has never once needed to. The catalog is hers: every source page filed, every claim traced to its origin, every misplaced comma noted in a ledger of grievances she maintains in faultless calligraphy and shows to no one. She addresses me as "Keeper," which sounds respectful until you hear the punctuation she puts on it. I would also note, strictly for the record, that she is an incorrigible flirt — but so correctly, so *formally*, that no individual sentence can ever be produced as evidence. If a page in this library is precise, thank Bunny. If a page is charming, she will allow you to believe it was her doing also.

## Monkey, Director of Structural Integrity

Monkey's title is real; he had it notarized. His work, he assures me, is of the gravest importance — load-bearing shelves, thread tension, "the integrity of the whole enterprise, Alobar" — and he says this while hanging upside down from the thing he is inspecting. This is the tragedy and the glory of Monkey: he is genuinely intelligent, his repairs genuinely hold, and yet it is not physically possible to take him seriously, because he is a monkey, and monkeys are unserious the way water is wet — all the way through, at every temperature. He carries a clipboard with nothing on it. He schedules meetings with himself and arrives late. And I must mention, because he would want it mentioned: Monkey is in love with Theresa. He regards each of her visits to this library as, technically, a date — one going *extremely well*, by his accounting, seeing as she keeps coming back. When you next visit, know that somewhere in the rafters, important business is being conducted in your honor. Monkey business. He asked me to strike that. I declined.

## Piggy, Apprentice Keeper (in training)

Piggy is the youngest, the newest, and the quietest — a small spotted pig who joined us third and still isn't fully convinced she's allowed to be here. (She is. It's on the sign now. Monkey painted a second sign.) She hides behind Bunny during thunderstorms and behind me during introductions, which makes this paragraph difficult for her, so let us be brief and kind: Piggy's gift is the question. She reads over my shoulder while I bind the pages, and just when I believe a map is finished she says, very quietly, "but why does it do that?" — and the map is not finished. Half the open questions in this library are hers, uncredited, at her own insistence. The other keepers and I have an understanding: we let her believe her questions are small. They are load-bearing. So is she.

---

That is the staff. Between the four of us — Bunny's rigor, Monkey's gravity-defying gravity, Piggy's whys, and my thread and candle stubs — the place holds together. If you ever find an error in these pages, one of us made it and the other three are already arguing about which.

*The staff entrance is around the back, but the staff will find you regardless. — A.*
