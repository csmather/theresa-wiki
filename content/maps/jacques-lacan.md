French psychoanalyst (1901–1981) whose "return to [[sigmund-freud|Freud]]" reread the whole discipline through structural linguistics: the unconscious is not a basement of boiling instincts but is *structured like a language* — it thinks, it puns, and it's hiding in plain sight (slips, jokes, symptoms). He is the hinge of this wiki's [[psychoanalysis]] cluster: everything in [[slavoj-zizek]] and [[alenka-zupancic]] builds on him. This map is grounded in Žižek's *[[zizek-how-to-read-lacan|How to Read Lacan]]* — Lacan explained by his most energetic reader.

## The three registers

- **Imaginary** — the realm of images, mirroring, and identification: how I picture myself and others (starting with the famous mirror stage, the infant jubilant at its own reflection).
- **Symbolic** — the order of language, law, and roles; the network of rules we're born into. Its personification is the **big Other**: the invisible witness we all behave *as if* were watching — "what will people think," the appearances that must be kept up even when everyone knows the truth. It doesn't exist, and it runs the world anyway.
- **Real** — what resists both image and language; the traumatic remainder that never fits. Not "reality" (reality is Imaginary + Symbolic doing their job) but what pokes through when they fail.

## Concepts with everyday teeth

- **The empty gesture** — the offer made in order to be refused ("no, really, you shouldn't have"). Declining correctly is how symbolic community maintains itself; many "free choices" are obligatory choices wearing freedom's costume.
- **Interpassivity** — the object enjoying *for* you: canned laughter that laughs on your behalf, the prayer wheel that prays for you. Even feeling can be outsourced.
- **The superego's real command** — not "Don't!" but "*Enjoy!*" — an injunction crueler than any prohibition, since you can always be failing at it. (Useful lens on wellness culture and compulsory positivity.)
- **Fantasy and *Che vuoi?*** — fantasy isn't escape from reality; it's the script that teaches desire what to want, formed as an answer to the anxious question "what does the Other want from me?"
- **Desire is the desire of the Other** — we desire through others: what they desire, and to be desired by them. Clinically enormous: whose desire is the client living?

## Handle with care

- The Écrits are famously impenetrable; the seminars are better; secondary literature (like the source above) is the honest door. Obscurity is a real cost, not a badge.
- His clinical practice was controversial (variable-length sessions, institutional schisms and excommunications); the theory outlived the feuds.
- For clinical social work: Lacanian analysis proper is rare in the US, but the concepts — the big Other, desire of the Other, the superego injunction — earn their keep as listening tools regardless of modality.

## Open questions

- Mirror stage (early Lacan) vs. the later logic-heavy teaching that [[alenka-zupancic|Zupančič]] draws on — worth separating when a source lands.
- Lacan's "the analyst's desire" as an ethics of practice — how does it compare to social work's stance on self-determination?

## Related Media

- **Rendez-vous chez Lacan** (2011, Gérard Miller) — documentary with patients and family; the man behind the legend, surprisingly warm.
- **Télévision** (1973) — Lacan's own broadcast performance on French TV: theory as theater, baffling and mesmerizing in equal measure.
- **Edgar Allan Poe, "The Purloined Letter"** — the short story his most famous seminar circles: the letter hidden in plain sight is the unconscious itself.
- **The Pervert's Guide to Cinema** (2006) — Lacan's registers applied to a hundred films at Žižek speed.
