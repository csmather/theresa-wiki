Ursula K. Le Guin's 1997 English *rendition* (her word — she read no classical Chinese) of the Tao Te Ching, made over four decades of returning to Paul Carus's 1898 character-by-character transliteration, with the scholar J. P. Seaton advising. Where most versions render Laozi as counsel for wise rulers, hers is addressed to an ordinary person — "a book about the way and the power of the way" for someone who still has to do the dishes.

## What she changed

- De-gendered the sage: no more "the Master, he." A small grammatical choice that completely shifts who the book is *for*.
- The register is plain, musical, and unsolemn; her footnotes are dry and occasionally very funny.
- She foregrounds the [[taoism|taoist]] core — water, yielding, the usefulness of what isn't there (the hub of the wheel, the hollow of the cup) — and mutes the statecraft-manual reading other translations lean on.

## Taoism runs through all her fiction

- *The Lathe of Heaven* (1971) — the title lifted from a nineteenth-century English mistranslation of Zhuangzi (she owned the error cheerfully when it was pointed out); a novel about the catastrophe of fixing the world by force. Wu wei as plot.
- Earthsea — a magic system that is explicitly Equilibrium: every act tips the Balance, so the wise act as little as possible.
- *The Left Hand of Darkness* — "Light is the left hand of darkness / and darkness the right hand of light": the yin-yang emblem as poetics, and as a whole planet's way of being.
- *The Dispossessed* — anarchism as political wu wei: order without a ruler.

## Why it's in this wiki

- The friendliest door into the [[taoism]] cluster — the literary sibling of [[fritjof-capra|Capra]]'s physics door and [[christopher-alexander|Alexander]]'s design door.
- Her ethic of non-coercion and her "carrier bag" sensibility (the hero as gatherer rather than conqueror) rhyme closely with [[anne-dufourmantelle]]'s gentleness-as-power.

## Open questions

- Set her rendition against a scholarly translation (D. C. Lau is the standard) — what does fidelity buy, and what does poetry buy?
- Her essay "The Carrier Bag Theory of Fiction" deserves its own map when a source lands — it's short and it rewires how you read stories.

## Related Media

- **Worlds of Ursula K. Le Guin** (2018, dir. Arwen Curry) — the documentary; she narrates her own late fame with perfect dryness.
- **Music and Poetry of the Kesh** (1985, with Todd Barton) — the album she recorded as an artifact of the invented culture in *Always Coming Home*: anthropology of a people who don't exist yet.
- **Fan Kuan, *Travelers Among Mountains and Streams*** (c. 1000) — the monumental Chinese landscape painting; the humans are tiny, and that's the point. The visual world the Tao Te Ching assumes.
- **Her 2014 National Book Awards speech** (six minutes, easy to find) — "We live in capitalism. Its power seems inescapable. So did the divine right of kings." The Tao Te Ching's politics, delivered in an evening gown.
- **The Lathe of Heaven** (the novel) — if fiction is the preferred door, skip straight to it.
