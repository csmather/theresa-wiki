Hub for this wiki's psychoanalysis cluster. The tradition's founding bet, still its live wire: we are not transparent to ourselves — and the opaque part isn't random noise but structured, patterned, and busy. Everything else (couches, jargon, schisms) is downstream implementation detail.

## The cluster, mapped

- [[sigmund-freud]] — the source: dynamic unconscious, transference, defense, repetition.
- [[ignacio-matte-blanco]] — the formalist: the unconscious as a *logic* (bi-logic), emotion as infinity.
- [[jacques-lacan]] — the hinge (no page yet): his "return to Freud" reads the unconscious as structured like a language, and the Ljubljana school builds directly on him.
- [[alenka-zupancic]] and [[slavoj-zizek]] — the philosophers: psychoanalysis as a tool for thinking ethics, comedy, sexuality, and ideology, not only a therapy.
- [[anne-dufourmantelle]] — the practicing analyst as philosopher: risk, gentleness, and the defended secret.

## Shared vocabulary worth owning

- **Unconscious** (dynamic — actively maintained, not merely un-noticed) · **transference / counter-transference** · **defense mechanisms** · **repetition compulsion** · **drive** (unlike an instinct, it has no fixed natural object) · **the symptom** — read as a message rather than a malfunction.

## Psychoanalysis and social work

- History: American casework absorbed psychoanalysis in the 1920s–40s (the "psychiatric deluge"); the diagnostic school of social work was essentially applied Freud.
- Living residue: psychodynamic listening, "use of self," supervision culture, and the attention to the relationship itself as the instrument of change.
- Standing critiques a practitioner should carry: pathologizing tendencies, cultural and gender bias baked into the classical texts, and a cost/length structure that historically made treatment a luxury good.

## Related Media

- **In Treatment** (HBO, 2008–) — one therapy session per episode; the best filmed depiction of transference and counter-transference actually operating.
- **Ordinary People** (1980) — grief, a family system, and a therapist who genuinely helps; still the reference portrayal.
- **The Sopranos** — seven seasons of a treatment that shouldn't work and somehow sort of does; also a masterclass in every defense mechanism.
- **The Freud Museum, London** — his actual study and the actual couch, preserved at 20 Maresfield Gardens; their online talks are good too.
