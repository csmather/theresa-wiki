## Summary

English translation (posted at Idiosophy, August 2017) of Anastasia Vécrin's September 2015 *Libération* interview with Anne Dufourmantelle about *Éloge du risque* (In Praise of Risk). Conducted in the wake of terror attacks and the security politics that followed, it's the most compact statement of her core argument in her own words: the zero-risk society is a fantasy that produces the fear it promises to cure, and being fully alive requires risk. The interview circulated widely after her death two years later, rescuing two children from dangerous surf.

## Key Claims

- "The idea of absolute security — 'zero risk' — is a fantasy. It appeals to an archaic sense of impotence."
- Protection backfires perceptually: "'Security' generates fear more than it assuages it... the signs of protection reactivate the feeling of insecurity."
- "To live is to take risks by definition... To be entirely alive is a risk, and few people are."
- Overprotection is political: "When we start from infantilization, we put ourselves under guardianship... a person with autonomy is less easy to influence than one governed by fear." Security discourse produces governable subjects.
- Vulnerability admitted is generative: "When one admits his fear, his finitude, a confidence can be reborn from this vulnerability."
- True protection isn't shelter from freedom: "True protection includes confidence in the capacity that someone does or doesn't have to experience their freedom."
- On borders and migrants: "It is our duty to open our borders, for the law of unconditional hospitality is the first humanizing rule of a civilization."
